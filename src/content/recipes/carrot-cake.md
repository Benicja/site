---
title: "Carrot Cake"
description: "A simplified, memorizable Swedish-style carrot cake that's light, fluffy, and perfect for kids and adults alike."
featured_image: "/images/recipes/carrot_cake_slice.jpg"
prep_time: 20
cook_time: 30
publishDate: 2026-02-06
category: "Dessert"
ingredients:
  - item: "Large carrots, grated (approx. 300g)"
    amount: "3"
  - item: "Eggs"
    amount: "3"
  - item: "Sugar"
    amount: "300 ml"
  - item: "Flour"
    amount: "300 ml"
  - item: "Baking powder"
    amount: "3 tsp."
  - item: "Rapeseed oil"
    amount: "33 ml"
  - item: "Philadelphia cheese"
    amount: "100 g"
  - item: "Softened butter"
    amount: "60 g"
  - item: "Icing sugar"
    amount: "400 ml"
  - item: "Limes (grated zest)"
    amount: "2"
instructions:
  - step: "Set the oven to 175°C (fan-assisted), otherwise 200°C, and line the bottom of a springform with a sheet of baking paper. You don't need to grease the sides."
  - step: "Peel and grate the carrots."
  - step: "Whisk the egg and sugar until fluffy with an electric whisk."
  - step: "Stir in the flour and baking powder and then the carrots and oil, mixing to a smooth batter."
  - step: "Pour the mixture into the tin and bake in the bottom half of the oven for approximately 30 minutes."
  - step: "Allow the cake to cool before loosening from the tin."
  - step: "Whisk the ingredients for the frosting with an electric whisk until smooth."
  - step: "Place the cake on a dish and spread the frosting over the cake."
---

Virtually all cafés in Sweden now serve carrot cake - perhaps it's the easiest way of getting our kids to eat root vegetables? I've simplified the recipe to make it easier to memorise.
